Perfect Cappuccino Recipe

  • 18g finely ground espresso
  • Fresh whole milk
  • Espresso machine
  • Milk pitcher
  • Coffee scale
  • Timer

Instructions

  1. Heat your espresso machine and cup. Proper temperature is crucial for the perfect shot.
  2. Grind 18g of coffee beans to a fine consistency. The grind should be slightly finer than table salt.
  3. Tamp the ground coffee evenly with about 30 pounds of pressure. Ensure the surface is level.
  4. Pull your espresso shot. Aim for a 25-30 second extraction time for 36g of espresso.
  5. While the shot is pulling, pour cold milk into your pitcher, filling it about 1/3 full.
  6. Steam your milk to create microfoam. The final temperature should be around 65°C (150°F).
  7. Tap the pitcher to remove any large bubbles and swirl to incorporate the foam.
  8. Pour the steamed milk into your espresso, starting from a height of about 3 inches.
  9. As the cup fills, bring the pitcher closer to create latte art if desired.

Pro Tips

Temperature Control

Keep your espresso machine at the right temperature (92-96°C) for optimal extraction.

Milk Texture

Create silky microfoam by keeping the steam wand just below the milk surface.

Timing

Aim for a 25-30 second extraction time for the perfect espresso shot.
